GCSE Pathways at Strathallan

Personalised academic pathways tailored to individual strengths

At Strathallan, we believe the best academic outcomes are achieved through a personalised approach to subject choices, balancing academic challenge with individual interests and strengths.

Most pupils study nine GCSEs in the Fourth and Fifth Forms, including core subjects of Mathematics, English Language and English Literature, at least one modern foreign language, and a minimum of two single-award sciences.

Our flexible curriculum is built around internationally respected qualifications, ensuring your child follows a pathway tailored to their abilities, ambitions, and aspirations.

Expert guidance ensures your child follows the pathway that best supports their ambitions. While most take Higher Tier courses (grades 9-4), Foundation Tier may suit some pupils better.

Three Distinct Pathways

9

Standard Pathway

The standard Strathallan curriculum of nine GCSEs provides a rigorous academic challenge and a strong foundation for progression to A Levels or Scottish Highers, ensuring a clear route to top universities.

9+

Extended Pathway

Exceptionally strong academic pupils may take an additional GCSE beyond the standard nine, pursuing a subject off the timetable out of genuine academic passion. Universities value high achievement in nine subjects over a larger number with lower grades.

8

Focused Pathway

For pupils who excel with a slightly reduced subject load, this option offers eight GCSEs with additional focus on achieving strong grades. Particularly suited to those with significant commitments in sport or performing arts, or who benefit from additional study support.

Our Boarding Houses

A Home Away From Home

A True Home from Home

We have seven senior boarding houses - four boys' and three girls' - plus Riley House for our junior boarders. Each house has its own unique feel and traditions, but all share a strong sense of family and welcome new pupils with open arms.

Day and boarding pupils are assigned to houses together and are equally encouraged to get involved in all aspects of school life. Unlike some schools, we do not allocate houses based on particular activities - instead we focus on creating a balance of interests and talents across all houses.

How House Allocation Works

Senior houses are allocated in the Summer Term prior to joining the senior school, and the allocation process is meticulously planned. We create a broad mix of abilities, interests and previous schools in each house. Sibling or family connections can sometimes influence preference to a particular house, but otherwise we focus on ensuring every house is balanced and welcoming.

The House Team

Housemaster/Housemistress

Lives in-house with their own family and is responsible for the wellbeing and safety of all house members.

Matron

Supervises the day-to-day running of the house, including cleaning and laundry, making sure everyone is happy and healthy.

House Tutors

Support pupils academically - monitoring progress, providing help where needed, and challenging your child to achieve their best.

Domestic Team

Keep bedrooms tidy, wash bedding and clothes, and maintain communal areas. They take a genuine interest in pupils' wellbeing and achievements.

House Facilities

Our boarding houses are designed with comfort and safety in mind - spaces where your child can unwind, relax with friends and focus on their studies.

Bedrooms

All girl boarders in the Senior School have their own single study bedroom. Boys are allocated single rooms from Fourth Form onwards. Day pupils share a study bedroom with another day pupil. Pupils are encouraged to decorate their rooms to help them feel at home.

Common Rooms

Each house has a central common room plus smaller year group common rooms for free time. These have comfortable sofas and entertainment options including TVs, board games and DVD libraries.

Kitchens & Social Spaces

Kitchens are social areas supplied with fresh bread and fruit daily so pupils can make snacks during evenings and weekends. Perfect for casual catch-ups with friends over a cup of tea or toast.

House Traditions & Spirit

Each house has its own special traditions - movie-marathon evenings, group baking sessions, fancy dress parties.

Houses compete in inter-house sports events and our annual House Music extravaganza, where each house puts together musical acts and battles it out to be crowned House Music champions.

Flexi-Boarding

Experience boarding life on your own terms

A Gentle Introduction

Flexi-boarding offers your child the chance to experience the benefits of boarding - independence, deeper friendships, evening activities, and homework support - while still returning home part of the week.

It's the perfect option for families who want their child to enjoy the social and academic benefits of boarding without committing to full-time from the start. Many flexi-boarders gradually increase their nights as they grow in confidence - some eventually choosing full boarding.

Flexi-Boarding Options

1 Night Per Week

2 Nights Per Week

Our most popular option. Enough time to settle in, join evening activities, and build friendships without being away too long.

3+ Nights Per Week

Almost full boarding. Ideal for older pupils who want more independence or families with busy weeknight schedules.

The Day Pupil Experience

No distinction - day pupils are fully part of Strathallan life

Fully Integrated

At Strathallan, we make no distinction between day pupils and boarders. Day pupils belong to a house, wear the same uniform, and have full access to every aspect of school life - from 7am breakfast through to evening prep and activities until 9:30pm.

This approach means day pupils form the same strong friendships, enjoy the same opportunities, and feel just as much a part of their house community as boarders. Many day families tell us their children don't want to come home!

Financial Assistance

Bursaries & Help with Fees

We believe the education we offer should be accessible to all. We provide a number of means-tested bursaries and fee remission policies to make this possible.

If you are considering Strathallan for your child, you are welcome to apply for a means-tested bursary. Whilst funds are limited, we are committed to helping as many pupils as possible, from all backgrounds, to experience a Strathallan education.

Bursaries are awarded independently of any scholarship or other award, but any pupil who is successfully awarded a scholarship may also apply for a bursary. They are made as a percentage discount of the full fee and are assessed according to the parents' ability to pay full fees, and the availability of bursary funds.

The bursary process is separate from admissions and financial background is not taken into account when awarding pupil places.

Our sibling fee remissions are not means-tested and available to all families at Strathallan. Percentage discounts are applied when siblings join their brothers or sisters at Strathallan.

Services/FCDO Remission

Currently serving members of HM Forces who are in receipt of the Continuity of Education Allowance (CEA) are eligible for a fee remission, meaning families would only be required to pay the minimum 10% parental contribution of the gross full boarding fee.

Strathallian Remission

A 5% discount on fees is available for children of Strathallians (former Strathallan pupils). This celebrates our alumni community and the continuing connection between generations.
